The Link Between Aging, NAD+, and Brain Decline

The human brain is an energy-hungry organ. Despite making up only 2% of body weight, it consumes about 20% of the body’s energy. This energy demand is powered by mitochondria—the “powerhouses” of cells—and regulated by NAD+.

Unfortunately, NAD+ levels decline as we age, leading to:

  • Reduced mitochondrial efficiency
  • Slower communication between brain cells
  • Higher risk of oxidative stress and inflammation
  • Accumulated DNA damage

The result? Brain fog, poor memory, slower processing speed, and increased vulnerability to neurodegenerative conditions like Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s disease.

By restoring NAD+ through NMN supplementation, you can help your brain maintain youthful energy and clarity.
